With OpenSim, we can study how we use our muscles to move and interact with the world. For example, how do we use our muscles to kick a soccer ball and score a goal?

Instructions
1) Use the sliders above to adjust the player's muscle forces:
- The quadriceps muscle is the large muscle in the front of your thigh. It extends or straightens your knee.
- The soleus muscle is in the back of your calf. You use it to point your toe.

2) Use the Turn slider adjust the rotation of the soccer player.

3) Once you pick the two muscle forces and the angle of your soccer player, press Kick. This will generate a simulation of the motion that results when the soccer player uses his muscles like you specified with the sliders.

Can you kick the ball past the goalie? How fast can you make the ball move? If you don't score the first time, readjust the sliders and try again.

Hints and Tips
Use the play button and slider at the top of the window to replay your motion in real time.

You can adjust the view window with your mouse:
- The left mouse button rotates the view.
- The middle mouse button lets you move the view up and down and side to side.
- The right mouse button zooms in and out.
